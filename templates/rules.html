ISL SNIC 2026 - Official Rulebook

Please read all rules carefully. Ignorance of any rule will not be accepted as justification for violations.

1. Definitions, Scope, and Authority

1.1 This document constitutes the complete and binding rulebook for the Swiss National Investment Championship at ISL (hereafter referred to as "the Competition").
1.2 The Competition is a simulated trading and investment event conducted for educational purposes only. No real financial assets are exchanged.
1.3 The Organizing Committee (hereafter referred to as "the Committee") retains full authority over the interpretation, enforcement, modification, and application of all rules.
1.4 By participating, all teams and individuals explicitly agree to comply with every rule listed in this document. Failure to comply will result in a violation ranging in severity (See clause 2).
1.5 The ignorance and/or ambiguity/misunderstanding of any rule shall not be accepted as justification for any violation.
1.6 The Committee reserves the right to issue binding clarifications that shall be considered extensions of this rulebook.

2. Severity Classification

All violations will be classified into one of the following categories:

Minor ViolationMINOR - A breach that does not affect fairness, safety, or integrity but violates procedural or formal rules.
Major ViolationMAJOR - A breach that affects competitive fairness, transparency, or proper conduct.
Critical ViolationCRITICAL - A breach that compromises the integrity, legality, or core principles of the Competition.
2.1 The Committee retains full discretion in classifying the severity of any violation.
2.2 Repeated Minor Violations (3) may be escalated to a Major Violation. Repeated Major violations (2) may be escalated to a Critical Violation (see clause 2.3).
2.3 Any single Critical Violation shall result in immediate disqualification unless explicitly stated otherwise.

3. Eligibility

3.1 All participants must be enrolled in MYP3 (Year 9) or above.
3.2 Participants below MYP3 may only compete if explicitly approved by their respective supervisor and the Committee.
3.3 The absolute minimum age for participation is 12 years.
3.4 Teams found to have falsified age or enrollment status shall be disqualifiedCRITICAL
3.5 Eligibility is checked at registration and may be rechecked at any time (see 12.4).

4. Team Composition

4.1 Each team must consist of no fewer than 2 and no more than 5 participants.
4.2 Teams outside this range shall be disqualifiedCRITICAL
4.3 Each team must designate:
  • One Team Leader (student)
  • One Supervisor (staff or teacher)
4.4 Only team leaders are permitted to manage the team's portfolio.
4.5 The Team Leader is responsible for internal coordination.
4.6 The Supervisor is responsible for compliance with this rulebook.
4.7 Failure to designate both roles constitutes a Major ViolationMAJOR

5. Supervisor Restrictions

5.1 Supervisors and all adults not enrolled as students must not provide specific trading, asset, or timing advice.
5.2 Only generic economic or financial education is permitted.
5.3 Any form of targeted strategic guidance constitutes a Critical ViolationCRITICAL
5.4 This rule includes indirect advice, hints, or recommendations.
5.5 Violations of this section override exemptions stated in 3.2.

6. Platform Usage

6.1 Only the officially provided trading platform may be used.
6.2 Personal accounts, shared accounts between teams, or third-party platforms are strictly prohibited.
6.3 All login credentials must remain confidential.
6.4 Any breach of account integrity constitutes a Critical ViolationCRITICAL
6.5 Teams are responsible for all activity conducted on their account (see 14.2).

7. Team Naming Rules

7.1 Team names must not be:
  • Racist and/or Discriminatory
  • Sexually explicit
  • Politically partisan
  • Religiously offensive
  • Violent
  • Extremist
  • Drug-related
  • Criminal
  • Harassing
  • Defamatory
  • Trademark-infringing
  • Referencing self-harm or suicide
7.2 Team names must not reference real brands, teams, or organizations.
7.3 Team names must be approved by the team's supervisor.
7.4 Team names must be approved by the Committee.
7.5 Inappropriate names result in immediate disqualificationCRITICAL
7.6 Renaming after approval is prohibited unless approved under 12.3.

8. Communication Restrictions

8.1 Team members may not communicate with other teams about strategy.
8.2 The only permitted external contact is the Competition Supervisor: Marco Bruce – mbruce@isl.ch, or the team supervisors.
8.3 Any attempt to coordinate strategies constitutes a Critical ViolationCRITICAL
8.4 Public forums, group chats, or social media discussions about strategy are prohibited.
8.5 Teams are permitted to appeal to any Violations placed upon them (See clause 2).

9. Cryptocurrency Ban

9.1 All forms of cryptocurrency trading are prohibited.
9.2 All crypto trackers, derivatives, or proxies are prohibited.
9.3 Attempting to simulate crypto exposure through ETFs or similar products constitutes a Critical ViolationCRITICAL

10. Trade Requirements

10.1 Each team must place at least one trade before March 9th.
10.2 Failure to do so constitutes a Critical ViolationCRITICAL
10.3 Every trade must include a written justification.
10.4 Trade justifications must be logical and written by team members.
10.5 Copying explanations constitutes a Major ViolationMAJOR
10.6 Missing explanations constitute a Minor ViolationMINOR

11. Legality and Ethics

11.1 All trading must comply with international trading laws, even in simulation.
11.2 Market manipulation is prohibited.
11.3 Insider trading is prohibited.
11.4 Any breach of this section constitutes a Critical ViolationCRITICAL

12. Enforcement and Audits

12.1 The Committee may audit any team at any time.
12.2 Teams must cooperate fully.
12.3 The Committee may require rule-based corrections.
12.4 Eligibility may be rechecked.
12.5 Refusal to comply constitutes a Critical ViolationCRITICAL

13. Account Access

13.1 Only registered team members may access the account.
13.2 Allowing others to trade is prohibited.
13.3 All actions taken on an account are the team's responsibility.
13.4 Breaches constitute a Critical ViolationCRITICAL

14. Anti-Loophole Clause

14.1 Any attempt to exploit ambiguities shall be treated as intentional misconduct and a Major ViolationMAJOR
14.2 The Committee's interpretation overrides all others.
14.3 If a rule does not explicitly allow an action, it is considered prohibited.
14.4 This clause supersedes all others.

15. Final Authority

15.1 The Committee's decisions are final.
15.2 Appeals may be submitted but may be rejected without explanation.
15.3 Participation implies full acceptance of this rulebook.

End of Official Rulebook

Grading System

Important Notes:
• A number in brackets (e.g., "(2)") indicates the number of marks for that sub-criteria
• 0 Marks in Criterion E will result in an end of marking for that team's work

Criterion A: Investment Performance 10 marks19.2%

ID Rubric Marks
A.1 Net positive gains in total money
The total money the team ends with is greater than the starting amount

Mark Allocations:
• 2 marks: Portfolio shows net positive return (any amount above starting capital)
• 1 mark: Portfolio breaks even or shows minimal loss (within -2% of starting capital)
• 0 marks: Portfolio shows significant loss (more than -2% below starting capital) 		2
A.2 Trade notes
The team's trade notes are relevant to the case study and market data (2), and allow for clear understanding of why the trade was made through logical explanation and reasoning (2)

Relevance (2 marks):
• 2 marks: All trades include notes with ticker symbol, position size, entry price, and specific rationale tied to market conditions
• 1 mark: Most trades have notes but some lack key details
• 0 marks: Trade notes are missing, incomplete, or not relevant

Clear Understanding (2 marks):
• 2 marks: Notes demonstrate deep understanding with specific catalysts, analysis, risk considerations
• 1 mark: Notes show basic understanding but lack specificity
• 0 marks: Notes show no real understanding or are absent 		4
A.3 Diverse investments
The trades made inside the team's portfolio are comprised of multiple different asset classes

Mark Allocations:
• 4 marks: Portfolio includes 4+ different asset classes
• 3 marks: Portfolio includes 3 different asset classes with meaningful allocation
• 2 marks: Portfolio includes 2 different asset classes
• 1 mark: Minimal diversification (mostly one asset class)
• 0 marks: Only 1 asset class or fewer than 3 total holdings 		4

Criterion B: Case Study Analytics 10 marks19.2%

ID Rubric Marks
B.1 Case study depth of research
The total extent of the depth of the team's research into companies and asset classes

Mark Allocations:
• 4 marks: Comprehensive research with 5+ credible citations, detailed financial metrics, competitor and industry analysis
• 3 marks: Good research with 3-4 citations, some financial analysis, multiple reliable sources
• 2 marks: Adequate research with 2 citations, basic company information
• 1 mark: Minimal research with 1 source, superficial analysis
• 0 marks: No meaningful research or unreliable sources 		4
B.2 Scenario analysis
The team thoroughly analyses the scenario of the case study with meaningful explanations

Mark Allocations:
• 2 marks: Thorough analysis of key risks/opportunities, market implications, specific strategy adjustments
• 1 mark: Basic scenario understanding but lacks depth or clear connection to strategy
• 0 marks: No meaningful scenario analysis or complete misunderstanding 		2
B.3 Adhering to case study
The team adheres to the case study by making meaningful trade notes linked to it (1) and making their trades coherent to it (3)

Trade Notes Linked (1 mark):
• 1 mark: Trade notes explicitly reference case study factors for most trades
• 0 marks: Notes fail to connect trades to case study

Trades Coherent (3 marks):
• 3 marks: 60%+ of portfolio value in case-study-aligned trades
• 2 marks: 40-59% in aligned trades, moderate coherence
• 1 mark: 20-39% in aligned trades, weak connection
• 0 marks: <20% aligned or directly contradicts case study 		4

Criterion C: Reports (Most Important) 16 marks30.8%

ID Rubric Marks
C.1 Research
The communication behind trades is understandable (3) and reinforced with relevant research (2)

Understandable Communication (3 marks):
• 3 marks: All explanations clear, logically organized, appropriate terminology with definitions
• 2 marks: Most explanations clear but some lack clarity
• 1 mark: Frequently unclear or overly technical
• 0 marks: Confusing or incoherent

Relevant Research (2 marks):

C.2 Documentation quality
Trade logs, portfolio records, and supporting materials are well-organized, professional, and complete

Mark Allocations:
• 3 marks: Complete documentation with all trade logs, portfolio snapshots, P&L calculations, supporting charts, professionally formatted
• 2 marks: Good documentation with minor gaps
• 1 mark: Basic documentation with significant gaps
• 0 marks: Documentation largely missing or disorganized 		3
C.3 Clarity and structure
Information is presented in a logical sequence with clear headings and professional formatting

Mark Allocations:
• 2 marks: Logical structure (executive summary → methodology → analysis → results → conclusion), clear headings, professional appearance
• 1 mark: Basic structure but inconsistent formatting
• 0 marks: Lacks clear structure or poor formatting 		2
C.4 Analysis and reflection
Critical evaluation of trading performance (1), risk assessment (1), and identification of lessons learned with actionable insights (1)

Mark Allocations:
• Performance evaluation (1): Honest critical analysis with specific examples and metrics
• Risk assessment (1): Discusses specific risks, volatility, position sizing
• Lessons learned (1): Identifies specific lessons with concrete actionable takeaways 		3
C.5 Technical accuracy
Correct use of financial terminology, accurate calculations, proper application of investment concepts (2), and appropriate citation of sources (1)

Mark Allocations:
• Investment concepts (2 marks): Consistently correct terminology, calculations, concept application
• Citation quality (1 mark): All sources properly cited with consistent format (Harvard - Cite Em Right style) 		3
